Hong Kong Borscht Soup

Description

This is a hearty soup from Hong Kong called borscht. It is packed full of veggies and has a nice meaty taste from beef shoulder. I like using beef shoulder because of how it falls off the bone after it's cooked, however, any beef joint will work for this recipe. This is perfect if you or your partner is ill and also works as a meal to keep you warm for the winter./p>

    Ingredients: (serves 4)
  • whole beef shoulder
  • 4 carrots
  • 4 white potatoes
  • 2 tomatoes
  • 3 stalks of celery
  • 2 ounches ginger
  • 2 bay leaves
    • Seasoning:
  • 5 tbsp ketchup
  • 500ml chicken stock
  • 1 tbsp salt
  • 2 tbsps sugar
  • 0.25 white pepper
    Steps:
  1. Fry the beef shoulder in the pan to brown the outside.
  2. Add the chicken stock in a pot with all the vegetables.
  3. Add the beef shoulder.
  4. Add the rest of the ingredients and seasoning.
  5. Partially cover the pot and simmer on low heat for 4 hours, topping up with water when necessary.
  6. Serve & enjoy!
